# Competitive Landscape — Incision Group B.V.

## 1. Company snapshot

Incision Group B.V. (incision.care) is an Amsterdam-headquartered digital surgical education and perioperative workflow company founded in 2014. Two product lines anchor the business:

- **Incision Academy** — accredited online surgical learning platform with 850+ multimedia courses, including step-by-step procedure walk-throughs filmed in OR/clinical settings, with 2D/3D anatomy and technique visualizations. The platform was first accredited as a Surgical Education Centre by the Royal College of Surgeons of England in April 2017 and reaccredited 2020–2023 ([Incision Academy](https://www.incision.care/products/academy); [RCS England](https://www.rcseng.ac.uk/education-and-exams/accreditation/accredited-centres/)).
- **Incision Assist** — perioperative workflow tool for OR teams covering procedure prep, instruments, and team coordination.

Series B closed November 2023, $7M, led by Alafi Capital (US) and Dennendonck (NL), with Rubio Impact Ventures and Invest International on the cap table ([Crunchbase round](https://www.crunchbase.com/funding_round/incision-series-b--417539f1); [ImpactAlpha](https://impactalpha.com/incision-raises-7-million-to-provide-online-training-to-global-surgical-teams/); [Invest International](https://investinternational.nl/news-items/invest-international-partners-with-incision-for-the-best-surgical-healthcare-worldwide/)). Roughly 85 FTE. Active US health-system pursuit (recent named targets per client briefing: University of Missouri Health, SSM, Mass General Brigham — not publicly confirmed contracts).

Incision sits at an unusual intersection: it is one of the very few accredited, full-perioperative-team focused content platforms (not just surgeons; explicitly OR nurses, surgical residents, scrub techs, the whole team), with a workflow-adjacent product. Most competitors target either surgeons-only or the device/AI layer.

## 2. Competitive universe — three rings

### Direct ring — digital surgical education / accredited surgical content

These compete head-on with Incision Academy.

- **Touch Surgery (Medtronic Digital Surgery)** — surgical procedure simulator + Touch Surgery Enterprise (AI video capture/analytics)
- **Osso VR** — VR-based surgical procedural training
- **FundamentalVR** — VR + haptics surgical training, ML insights
- **PrecisionOS** — VR orthopedic-led surgical training (small but growing)
- **Lecturio Medical** — broader medical education, with a surgical track (adjacent-direct)
- **Royal College of Surgeons (RCS), American College of Surgeons (ACS), AAOS** — society programs running their own digital course catalogs (incumbent-direct)

### Adjacent ring — surgical workflow software, OR analytics, AI-assisted coaching

Don't compete for the same content dollars but compete for the same OR-leadership budget and same buyer attention. They're also the most likely consolidators or acquirers.

- **Caresyntax** — surgical workflow + analytics platform, ~4,000 ORs deployed
- **Proximie** — surgical telepresence, recording, content library
- **Theator** — AI surgical video intelligence and post-op coaching
- **Activ Surgical** — intraoperative AI/visualization (ActivSight)
- **Avail Medsystems** (assets acquired by Mendaera) — virtual OR / remote presence
- **CrowdOptic** — surgical broadcasting / tele-mentoring
- **AISAP / DocBox** — OR data and device-integration platforms

### Incumbent ring — what budget gets displaced

- **Textbook publishers** — Elsevier (which owns Osmosis), Wolters Kluwer (UpToDate, Lippincott), Springer
- **Society in-person CME** — ACS, AAOS, SAGES, AORN annual meetings and skills labs
- **Strategic medtech educational arms** — Ethicon Institute (J&J), Medtronic Academia, Stryker (IRCAD partnership 2024 — Charlotte NC center), Intuitive (da Vinci training)
- **Hospital LMS** — HealthStream, Relias (cover OR nurse compliance content but not procedural depth)

## 3. Per-competitor profiles

### Touch Surgery / Medtronic Digital Surgery

- **What:** On-demand surgical simulator (mobile + web) with hundreds of procedure videos and walkthroughs; Touch Surgery Enterprise adds AI-powered intra-op video capture and analytics.
- **Modality:** Video + 2D/3D simulation; AI video analytics for Enterprise.
- **Accreditation:** Various CME partnerships; not RCS-accredited as a centre.
- **Buyer:** Surgeons individually (free tier); hospitals/IDNs for Enterprise.
- **Last funding event:** Acquired by Medtronic Feb 2020, terms undisclosed ([Medtronic press release](https://news.medtronic.com/2020-02-13-Medtronic-to-Advance-Solutions-and-Capabilities-in-Surgical-Data-and-Analytics-with-Acquisition-of-Digital-Surgery); [Fierce Biotech](https://www.fiercebiotech.com/medtech/medtronic-to-bolster-its-ai-and-surgical-robotics-work-digital-surgery-acquisition)).
- **Threat profile:** High. Strategic distribution muscle, bundled with Hugo RAS robot and Medtronic device portfolio. Surgeon-only focus (a gap vs. Incision's team focus).

### Osso VR

- **What:** VR procedural training across orthopedics, spine, cardio, and others.
- **Modality:** Immersive VR (Quest hardware).
- **Buyer:** Medical device manufacturers (largest revenue), residency programs, hospitals.
- **Last funding event:** $66M Series C, March 2022, led by Oak HC/FT; Tiger Global, SignalFire, GSR, Kaiser Permanente Ventures participated. Total raised $109M ([Fierce Healthcare](https://www.fiercehealthcare.com/health-tech/osso-vr-grabs-66m-scale-virtual-reality-surgical-training-tech); [PRNewswire](https://www.prnewswire.com/news-releases/osso-vr-raises-66-million-to-broaden-access-to-surgical-education-for-healthcare-professionals-301509932.html)).
- **Threat profile:** High in surgeon-skill-acquisition, low in non-surgeon roles. VR hardware barrier limits OR-team penetration.

### FundamentalVR

- **What:** VR + haptic surgical training; "Fundamental Surgery" platform with ML data layer.
- **Last funding event:** $20M Series B, Aug 2022, led by EQT Life Sciences. Total ~$30M+ ([MobiHealthNews](https://www.mobihealthnews.com/news/surgical-training-platform-fundamentalvr-scores-20m); [FundamentalVR](https://fundamentalsurgery.com/company-updates/fvr-raises-20-million/)).
- **Threat profile:** Medium. Strong haptics differentiation, smaller commercial footprint than Osso, primarily UK/Europe.

### Proximie

- **What:** Connected-OR platform — telepresence, recording, content/replay library, analytics.
- **Last funding event:** $80M Series C, June 2022, led by Advent Life Sciences; Emerson Collective, SoftBank Vision Fund 2, British Patient Capital, Mubadala participated. Total $140M ([PRNewswire](https://www.prnewswire.com/news-releases/proximie-raises-80-million-in-series-c-funding-to-accelerate-product-expansion-of-full-service-connected-surgical-platform-301567075.html)).
- **Threat profile:** High. Overlapping value proposition for "OR knowledge transfer" and is much better capitalized. Different modality (live presence > pre-recorded curriculum) but increasingly blurs into education library territory.

### Theator

- **What:** AI surgical video intelligence — automated indexing, post-op coaching, critical-view-of-safety detection. Won 2024 SAGES CVS challenge at MICCAI.
- **Last funding event:** Series A extension to $39.5M total, July 2022, led by Insight Partners; Mayo Clinic, Blumberg, NFX participated. Total disclosed ~$42.5M ([TechCrunch](https://techcrunch.com/2022/07/22/theator-an-ai-platform-that-analyzes-surgery-videos-closes-out-its-series-a-at-39-5m/); [PRNewswire](https://www.prnewswire.com/news-releases/theator-extends-series-a-funding-with-additional-24m-for-a-total-of-39-5m-301591753.html)).
- **Threat profile:** Medium-high. AI-coaching layer is a credible substitute for some passive video-curriculum hours and is the buzziest segment with investors.

### Caresyntax

- **What:** Surgical-intelligence/workflow platform — OR data ingest, analytics, surgical video, integration. ~4,000 ORs globally.
- **Last funding event:** $180M Series C extension, Aug 2024 ($80M equity + up to $100M growth debt). Investors include Symbiotic Capital, MTIP, BIONIQ, PFM Health Sciences, surgical.ai, BlackRock Innovation Capital, Optum Ventures, Lauxera, Vesalius. ~75% H1 2024 revenue growth, >50% organic FY24 growth ([Caresyntax press release](https://caresyntax.com/news/caresyntax-raises-180-million-funding-to-accelerate-growth-and-adoption-of-precision-surgery-2/); [Fierce Healthcare](https://www.fiercehealthcare.com/ai-and-machine-learning/precision-surgery-ai-company-caresyntax-banks-180m-accelerate-growth-and); [BusinessWire](https://www.businesswire.com/news/home/20240815975998/en/surgical.ai-Backs-Caresyntax-in-%24180M-Series-C-Extension-to-Accelerate-Adoption-of-AI-enabled-Surgery)).
- **Threat profile:** Highest in adjacent ring. Same hospital-IT buyer, much larger footprint, and is the named segment consolidator (M&A strategy explicit).

### Activ Surgical

- **What:** Intraoperative AI visualization (ActivSight); "ActivEdge" hardware-agnostic surgical software.
- **Last funding event:** $45M Series B; total raised ~$100M across 7 rounds ([Rising Tide](https://rtf.vc/blogs/our-investment-in-realtime-surgical-assistance-activ-surgical-raises-45-million-in-series-b-financing-to-advance-autonomous-and-collaborative-surgery/); [PitchBook](https://pitchbook.com/profiles/company/230540-23)).
- **Threat profile:** Low for education; adjacent device-software player, not a content competitor.

### Avail Medsystems

- **What:** Virtual OR / remote presence platform. Series B $73M; total ~$100M. **Status: virtual-OR assets acquired by Mendaera in 2025**, signaling stand-alone tele-presence didn't reach scale ([Crunchbase](https://www.crunchbase.com/organization/avail-medsystems/financial_details)).
- **Threat profile:** Low — case study in how hard the standalone remote-OR model is.

### Lecturio Medical

- **What:** Broad medical education platform (USMLE prep, basic + clinical sciences); used by 600+ medical schools. Surgical track is a slice. Funding not publicly detailed; remains private and is part of Lecturio GmbH.
- **Threat profile:** Medium for student/resident eyeballs, low for hospital/perioperative team contracts.

### AMBOSS

- **What:** Medical knowledge base + question bank; physician + student.
- **Last funding event:** Total $322M raised; investors include KIRKBI, Lightrock, M&G, Burda, Project A ([PitchBook](https://pitchbook.com/profiles/company/170011-18)).
- **Threat profile:** Low-medium. Strong adjacency (could move into surgical content library), but historically focused on knowledge/board prep rather than skill-building procedure walkthroughs.

### Osmosis (Elsevier)

- **What:** Visual medical education; acquired by Elsevier Nov 2021. Pre-acquisition raise was modest (~$9M) — strategic, not financial, exit ([PitchBook](https://pitchbook.com/profiles/company/97056-91)).
- **Threat profile:** Low directly; signal value high — confirms publishers are willing to acquire content platforms.

### Strategic-incumbent education arms

- **Ethicon Institute (J&J MedTech)** — open + MIS surgical training; physical centers + digital. J&J launched Polyphonic, an open digital surgical ecosystem, including video/telepresence/planning apps. Ottava soft-tissue robot in human trials April 2025 ([MedTech Dive](https://www.medtechdive.com/news/4-medtech-topics-trends-2026/809091/)).
- **Medtronic** — Touch Surgery (above) + Medtronic Academia centers, integrated with Hugo RAS.
- **Stryker** — IRCAD North America partnership announced 2024, opening Charlotte NC training center summer 2025; covers robotics, VR/AR, surgical AI, simulation.
- **Intuitive Surgical** — da Vinci certification curriculum; deepest moat in robotic-assisted training but proprietary to da Vinci.

### Society / academic

- **RCS England, RCS Edinburgh, ACS, AAOS, SAGES, AORN** — accredited course catalogs and skills meetings. Move slowly, low product velocity, but own the credentialing rails. Note: Incision's RCS England Surgical Education Centre accreditation is the differentiator here.

## 5. White-space — where Incision is genuinely differentiated

1. **Multi-role perioperative team focus.** Touch Surgery, Osso, FundamentalVR, Theator are surgeon-centric. OR nurses, scrub techs, surgical residents, and the broader team have no comparable accredited content library at this depth. This is a real gap; it maps to a different buyer (the OR director / CNO / perioperative VP), and it's where US health-system pursuits should anchor.
2. **RCS England Surgical Education Centre accreditation.** No other independent digital platform of comparable size carries a Royal College centre accreditation (vs. one-off course CME). For US health systems this is a credentialing differentiator with European pedigree.
3. **Workflow-adjacent (Incision Assist).** Most pure-play education vendors don't have a workflow product; most workflow vendors (Caresyntax, Proximie) don't have a 850-course accredited library. Bridging both into a single perioperative SKU is unusual.
4. **Capital efficiency / device-agnostic.** Unlike Medtronic Touch Surgery or Stryker/IRCAD, Incision is not tethered to a single device manufacturer's procedures. For a hospital running mixed Medtronic+J&J+Stryker ORs, this is a buyer-neutral advantage.

## 6. Threat analysis — top 5

1. **Caresyntax** — best-funded adjacent player, explicit M&A acquirer mode after Aug 2024 round, same hospital perioperative-ops buyer. Could either acquire Incision or out-bundle it. Highest competitive heat.
2. **Medtronic Touch Surgery** — strategic distribution muscle, free tier as wedge, increasingly bundled with Hugo. Risk: surgeons get "free, good enough" library inside Medtronic ecosystem before Incision lands the same site.
3. **Proximie** — content library is a side-effect of telepresence platform, but at $140M raised they can build curriculum at speed. If they pivot toward team-training packaging, direct overlap.
4. **J&J Polyphonic ecosystem** — newer, broader strategic platform; J&J's Ethicon Institute footprint plus open-architecture digital play makes them a credible 2026–2027 entrant in team education.
5. **Society digital programs (RCS, ACS, AAOS, SAGES)** — slow but own the credentialing rails. A "good enough" subsidized digital program from any major society could erode willingness-to-pay for an independent vendor.

## 7. Strategic acquirer map — 3-5 named

For Series C horizon (12–18 months) and beyond, the most plausible strategic acquirers, ranked by fit:

1. **Medtronic** — already owns Touch Surgery; has the deepest internal sense of what a content+workflow asset is worth. Incision adds non-surgeon team content (gap in Touch Surgery's catalog) and EU healthcare relationships. Past deal pattern (Digital Surgery 2020) shows willingness to roll up education+AI assets.
2. **Johnson & Johnson MedTech (Ethicon)** — Polyphonic open-ecosystem strategy needs content depth and a non-J&J-tethered training story; Ottava launch creates a 2026–2027 training surge. Plausible content+workflow tuck-in.
3. **Stryker** — fresh IRCAD partnership signals appetite for education at scale. Less obvious software DNA, but Mako/MIS portfolio expansion needs digital training rails.
4. **Caresyntax** — explicitly acquisitive after Aug 2024 round. Curriculum library + workflow product is precisely the kind of asset they've talked about. Most likely *adjacent* (not strategic medtech) acquirer.
5. **Elsevier / Wolters Kluwer** — publishers buying their way into procedural visual content (Osmosis precedent for Elsevier). Lower probability but a real fallback path; would value content + accreditation more than workflow.

Lower-probability mentions: GE HealthCare, Becton Dickinson, hospital-IT vendors (Epic, Oracle Health, Workday Healthcare) — generally don't acquire clinical content vendors directly, but worth tracking if they shift.
