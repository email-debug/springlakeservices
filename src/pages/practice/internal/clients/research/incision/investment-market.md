Incision is in commercial-scaling mode after a $7M Series B (Nov 2023, led by Alafi Capital + Dennendonck) and is not currently raising. This memo establishes: (a) the comparable round set in surgical training / perioperative software / digital surgery, (b) the lead-investor inventory we'd target, (c) plausible round size and valuation for an Incision Series C, and (d) the temperature of the segment.

For a US-led Series C we should size against companies one full stage ahead of where Incision's headcount (~85 FTE) and revenue base would otherwise place them.

## 5. Term-norm summary (US-led Series C in healthtech, 2025–2026 environment)

Standard terms we'd expect — calibrated to current digital-health Series C norms, not 2021 froth:

- **Option pool:** 10–12.5% post-money, refreshed pre-money to lead's diligence.
- **Board:** 5-seat board typical at C — 2 founder/common, 2 investor (1 Series C lead + 1 from Series A/B), 1 independent. If a strategic comes in, often a sixth observer rather than full seat.
- **Liquidation preference:** 1× non-participating preferred is the norm. Anything stacked or participating is a flag the deal is being marketed at a stretched valuation.
- **Anti-dilution:** Broad-based weighted-average is standard; full-ratchet would be unusual outside of a down-round structure.
- **Pro-rata rights:** Standard for major investors; super-pro-rata occasionally extended to a strategic.
- **Information rights, ROFR/co-sale, drag-along:** Standard 2026 NVCA-flavored.
- **Dutch-specific note:** If Incision keeps Dutch B.V. as the financing entity, expect US lead investors to require either a US Inc. flip or a custom Dutch-law SHA. The flip question is non-trivial and should be staged before the round, not during.

## 6. Market temperature read — surgical edtech / OR software

**Read: warming, not hot. Selectively hot for AI-anchored stories.**

- 2024–2025 digital health funding rebounded materially. US digital health 2025 reached ~$14.2B, +35% over 2024; healthcare AI alone took ~$3.95B in H1 2025, ~62% of digital health spend ([Fierce Healthcare](https://www.fiercehealthcare.com/health-tech/healthcare-ai-rakes-nearly-4b-vc-funding-buoying-digital-health-market-2025)).
- 2024 median Series C $50M, Series D $55M — down from 2021 peaks ($73M / $105M) but stabilized ([Rock Health](https://rockhealth.com/insights/2024-year-end-market-overview-davids-and-goliaths/) / [Rock Health 2025 YE](https://rockhealth.com/insights/2025-year-end-digital-health-funding-overview-a-tale-of-two-markets/)).
- Within the surgical-tech sub-segment specifically, the marquee 2024 round was Caresyntax's $180M ext. — the proof point that the segment can absorb a real growth-equity-sized check, but with debt as a meaningful share. No comparable surgical-education-only Series C has closed in 2024–2025; Osso VR and Proximie last raised in 2022 and have stayed quiet.
- The signal: **growth investors will lean in for surgical-software stories that have either (a) AI-coaching/intelligence framing or (b) demonstrable hospital-deployment scale.** Pure content-library narratives without one of those two is harder.
- Recent passes (anecdotal, not sourced) suggest investors are particularly skeptical of VR-hardware-dependent training stories after Avail Medsystems' wind-down to Mendaera and the slower-than-expected Quest enterprise penetration.

## 7. Series C scenario for Incision (12–18 months out)

Given (a) the small Series B size, (b) ~85 FTE base, (c) US health-system pursuit narrative, and (d) the 2024–2026 digital-health Series C medians around $50M, our best-honest framing for the engagement:

**Scenario A — "true Series C, US-led" ($30–50M raise, $150–250M post-money):**
Requires Incision to land 2–3 named US health-system contracts (LOI or signed) by raise time and demonstrate ARR in the $8–15M range with credible >70% YoY growth. Lead would most plausibly be Oak HC/FT, Advent, or Insight; strategic from Optum Ventures or a medtech CVC alongside.

**Scenario B — "European-led Series B+/C, smaller bridge" ($15–25M raise, $80–140M post-money):**
If US traction is uneven by raise time, more likely an EU-anchored round led by Lauxera, MTIP, or EQT Life Sciences with US strategic participation. Less aspirational but more probable absent step-function commercial proof.

**Scenario C — strategic acquisition in lieu of Series C (see competitive-landscape memo Section 7):**
If US health-system pursuits open up M&A interest from Medtronic / J&J Ethicon / Stryker / Caresyntax, the company may face a buy-vs-raise decision rather than a clean financing event. Caresyntax's 2024 M&A posture is the most concrete signal here.

**Most likely path (analyst inference, not sourced):** Scenario A or B with a strategic CVC in the syndicate. We should be running both rails in parallel as we engage.

## 8. Strategic vs. financial capital — which fits Incision better?

The question we'd put to Eric for partner-level calibration: with Caresyntax/Proximie/Touch Surgery as guideposts, is Incision's natural next round a financial Series C or a strategic round/acquisition?

- **Financial case:** Surgical edtech with team-training white space and RCS accreditation has a clean pure-play story. Oak HC/FT and Insight have funded analogous theses. Cap-table independence preserves optionality for a higher-ceiling exit later.
- **Strategic case:** Touch Surgery sold to Medtronic in 2020 at a stage not far from where Incision will be. J&J Polyphonic and Stryker/IRCAD show strategics building education ecosystems that lack a multi-role team-content backbone — exactly Incision's white space. Caresyntax is in active M&A roll-up mode and would value the asset.
- **Practice recommendation (subject to Eric review):** Run a financial-led round with at least one strategic CVC in the syndicate (Optum Ventures, J&J Innovation, or Medtronic Ventures) so the Series C round itself qualifies the company for an inbound 18-month later. Avoid a single strategic lead — that prices an acquisition into the round terms.
